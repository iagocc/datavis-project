A little about this project

@andreluiscm and @iagocc on December 04, 2018

This website aims to show you interactive results of a survey proposed by Mozilla. In this data exploration task we seek to understand the behavior of internet users spread the world related to their affinities and expectations about technological stuff.

This project is a final assessment of a Data Visualization Course of the Departament of Computer Science at the Federal University of Ceará. Almost all knowledge applied here was presented at the course.

The data has more than 130 Mb, 53 columns and 192k lines of CSV file. Each line of the data represent an individual user's answer. It was collected throughout five months of 2017.

André and Iago are PhD Students on Computer Science at the Federal University of Ceará.

People around the world who attended the survey

You can select by clicking in your prefered country to adjust the three following charts. The selected country is represented by a red coloring.

The map shows the distribution of users who attended the survey. Each country has a color accordingly to the number of user who attended the survey and belongs to it.

Different technology tastes by Country

The chart compares the selected country taste rates with the global ones. The tastes are closely related to the user's desire of pursuing a smart device.

Density-based user's priorities representation for purchasing a smart device by Country

The chart shows the density representation of what the users consider to be more important when they buy a smart device.

User's known technologies by Country

This representation shows what the respondents are capable to argue or explain for someone else. We can compare the result by a selected country with the overall mean.

Attendances per Day

This chart below can be brushed to update the following pie chart.

The line chart shows the attendance by day. It is important to notice that the vertical axis is in logarithmic form to get a better scale.
